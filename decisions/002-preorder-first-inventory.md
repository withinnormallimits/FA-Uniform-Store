# 002 — Inventory model: preorder-first hybrid

**Decision:** Sell decorated/logo items only through announced preorder windows (order → bulk PO → batch distribution), and keep stock-on-hand only for fast-moving basics and used uniforms.

**Alternatives considered:**
- Full stock-on-hand store — maximum immediacy, but dead-stock risk on unreturnable decorated goods, storage the school doesn't have, and size-guessing.
- Pure preorder for everything — zero inventory risk, but parents can't buy a replacement polo in October.
- Fully vendor-outsourced (FlynnO'Hara/Lands' End model) — zero labor, but hands parents the exact delay/stockout misery Lane A documented.

**Why this won:** The preorder window is the proven small-school pattern that eliminates storage, dead stock, and demand-guessing (Lane G), while a small basics stock answers the mid-year replacement need that pure preorder fails; decorated goods are final-sale and lead-time-doubled in summer (Lane F), which makes stocking them the single worst financial exposure.

**Reverse if:** Two seasons of sell-through data make demand predictable enough to stock decorated goods safely, or a local decorator offers small-batch turnaround that removes the bulk-PO constraint.
