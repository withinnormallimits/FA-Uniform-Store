# Lane C — Platform Options

Research date: **July 2026.** All prices USD, US offerings unless flagged. Direct fetches of several official pricing pages were bot-blocked (403), so some figures come from recent third-party pricing roundups; each is flagged. **Verify final numbers on official pricing pages before deciding.**

## 1. Hosted e-commerce platforms

| Platform / plan | Monthly cost | Processing / transaction fees | Notes for a school store |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Shopify Starter** | $5/mo | 5% + 30¢ online; +5% if third-party gateway | Link-in-bio/simple storefront only — no full online store; too thin for a uniform shop |
| **Shopify Basic** | $29/mo annual · $39/mo monthly | 2.9% + 30¢ (Shopify Payments); +2% if third-party gateway | Full store, inventory, local pickup, customer accounts, built-in password page |
| **Square Online — Free** | $0 | 3.3% + 30¢ online (2.6% + 15¢ in person) | Free tier is real; higher online rate is the tradeoff |
| **Square — Plus** | $49/mo | 2.9% + 30¢ online | Square unified its tiers (Free/Plus $49/Premium $149) in its 2025 repricing |
| **BigCommerce Standard ("Core" since 6/1/2026)** | $39/mo · $29/mo annual | 0% platform fee; processor rate only (e.g., PayPal/Braintree ~2.9%+30¢) | Forced upgrade to Plus/"Growth" at $50K trailing-12-mo sales — irrelevant at school scale |
| **Ecwid (Lightspeed) Venture** | ~$15–25/mo (repriced 3/2/2026 — confirm) | 0% platform fee; bring your own gateway (Stripe/PayPal/Square) | Embeds a store into ANY existing site (incl. the school's current site); Venture ≈100 products |
| **Ecwid Business** | ~$45–49/mo (confirm) | same | ~2,500 products; app market access |

- Shopify pricing: [Shopify pricing roundups, 2026](https://www.demandsage.com/shopify-pricing/), [cybernews](https://cybernews.com/best-website-builders/shopify-review/pricing/) (third-party; official page shopify.com/pricing was bot-blocked).
- Square tiers/fees: [posusa.com](https://www.posusa.com/square-fees-pricing/), [merchantinsiders.com](https://merchantinsiders.com/blogs/square-fees/), [NerdWallet](https://www.nerdwallet.com/business/software/learn/square-fees). ACH via Square invoices: 1% ($1 min) — useful for large uniform orders.
- BigCommerce: [costbench](https://costbench.com/software/ecommerce/bigcommerce/), [ecommerceparadise](https://ecommerceparadise.com/bigcommerce-pricing-plans-2026-complete-cost-breakdown/) — plan renames June 2026, prices unchanged.
- Ecwid: repricing effective Mar 2, 2026 per [Ecwid Help Center](https://support.ecwid.com/hc/en-us/articles/25122701806108-Changes-to-the-Ecwid-plan-pricing-after-March-2-2026); third-party sources conflict on new numbers ([tooltester](https://www.tooltester.com/en/reviews/ecwid-review/pricing/), [litextension](https://litextension.com/blog/ecwid-pricing/)) — **treat exact Ecwid prices as unconfirmed**.

**School-relevant features**
- **Gated storefront:** Shopify — built-in storewide password page ([Shopify Help](https://help.shopify.com/en/manual/online-store/themes/password-page)); finer gating (per-family pricing, login-to-view) needs ~$5–15/mo apps like BSS B2B Lock / LockPro ([Shopify App Store](https://apps.shopify.com/login-to-access-pages)). Square Online — page-level passwords on standard pages only; **cannot password-protect the main Shop All/category system pages** ([Square Support](https://squareup.com/help/us/en/article/7047-password-protection), [community thread](https://community.squareup.com/t5/Online-Store/password-protect-shopall-page/m-p/828292)); "hidden items" workaround exists ([Square Support](https://squareup.com/help/us/en/article/6881-sell-hidden-items-in-square-online-store)). Ecwid — no native store password; "Required Login for Storefront Access" app forces customer sign-in ([Ecwid app](https://www.ecwid.com/apps/tools/required-login)). BigCommerce — customer-group gating is a genuine strength but overkill here.
- **Local pickup + inventory:** all four support pickup fulfillment and per-item inventory tracking on paid plans (Shopify/Square/BigCommerce official help docs).
- **Expansion path:** Shopify/BigCommerce = commerce only (no tuition/forms/events); Square is the standout — same account can add invoices, appointments, ACH, POS for the front office. Ecwid rides on whatever site the school already has, so it doesn't box you in structurally.

## 3. School-specific vendors

**Payment/SIS platforms (school-managed inventory, parents pay fees on top):**
- **MySchoolBucks (Heartland/Global Payments)** — school store module lets schools sell items/fees; parents pay program fees of roughly **3.8%–4.95% per transaction** depending on district agreement (examples: [ISD 411 3.8% notice](https://www.isd411.org/about-us/announcements/single-announcement/~board/district-announcements/post/myschoolbuckscom-announces-fall-2023-program-fee), [Hopkinton fee page](https://www.hopkinton.k12.ma.us/parents-students-welcome/fees-payments), [MySchoolBucks FAQ](https://www.myschoolbucks.com/ver2/help/gethelp.action)). Fee examples are district-specific and some notices date to 2023 — **partially stale; get a quote**. Built for K-12 fee collection, not merchandising (no real product pages/variants/inventory UX).
- **FACTS (Nelnet) Incidental Billing** — if the school already uses FACTS for tuition, one-off charges (uniforms, supplies, aftercare, trips) can be posted to family accounts; ACH free to families, **2.85% card fee** ([FACTS incidental billing](https://factsmgt.com/features/accounting-billing-payments/), [school flyer example](https://sacredheartva.org/wp-content/uploads/FACTS-Incidental-Billing-Flyer-1.pdf) — flyer fee figure may predate 2025, confirm). It's billing, not a storefront: no browsing/cart; school manages inventory and order-taking elsewhere.
- **TADS (VenturEd Solutions/Community Brands)** — whole-school billing incl. incidentals (lunch, sports, trips, fees) attached to invoices ([TADS billing FAQ](https://www.tads.com/faq/billing-tuition-management/), [product page](https://www.tads.com/product/tuition-and-billing-management/)). Same caveat: invoicing engine, not a store. Pricing is quote-based.
- **Jackrabbit Class** — $49–245/mo by student count ([official pricing](https://www.jackrabbitclass.com/pricing/)); built for gyms/dance/swim class businesses, not private-school retail — **poor fit**, listed for completeness.

**Vendor-fulfilled apparel (zero school inventory, revenue-share):**
- **SquadLocker** — free store; ~20K decoratable items; school sets 5/10/15% markup, **100% of markup back to school**; payout by check monthly after store close ([SquadLocker fundraising blog — vendor marketing](https://www.squadlocker.com/squad-blog/fundraising-made-easy-via-your-squadlocker-store), [markup help article](https://help.squadlocker.com/hc/en-us/articles/115010679307-Do-we-set-the-price-or-is-it-fixed-)). Tradeoff: vendor sets base prices (often high), print-on-demand quality/turnaround varies.
- **1st Place Spiritwear** — free always-open store, on-demand DTG printing, no minimums; **~10% of gross sales back to school** ([1stplacespiritwear.com/about — vendor marketing](https://1stplacespiritwear.com/about), [FAQ](https://1stplacespiritwear.com/faq)). Spirit wear only — not dress-code uniforms.
- **BSN Sports "My Team Shop" / Fanwear Shop** — free team/fan stores, school chooses lower prices vs. bigger fundraising cut; "no hidden fees" ([BSN team shop — vendor marketing](https://www.bsnsports.com/solutions/team-shop/), [fanwear](https://www.bsnsports.com/solutions/fanwear-shop/)). Athletics-oriented catalogs.
- **EduKit / supply-kit vendors (SchoolKidz, 1st Day School Supplies)** — school submits teacher lists → vendor quotes per-kit price → parents order in a seasonal window → kits shipped free to school; school can add a per-kit markup as fundraiser ([EduKit how-it-works — vendor marketing](https://www.edukitinc.com/blog/how-does-the-edukit-program-work), [FAQ](https://www.edukitinc.com/faqs)). Seasonal only — solves supplies, not the year-round store.
- **Common thread:** vendor-fulfilled = no cost, no inventory, no labor, but no control over pricing/quality/catalog, revenue share only ~5–15%, and **school-provided products (used uniforms, required items the school stocks) can't flow through them.** None expands to tuition/forms/events.

## 4. Custom builds & low-code

- **Custom web app (Stripe + hosting):** freelance build for a startup-level store realistically **$8K–20K** up front (freelancers $25–75/hr; boutique agencies $75–120/hr), incl. Stripe/PayPal, SSL, CMS; maintenance **15–25% of build cost/yr** ([fyresite 2026 breakdown](https://www.fyresite.com/how-much-does-an-ecommerce-website-cost-to-develop/), [digitalapplied](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/website-development-cost-2026-complete-pricing-data)). Stripe standard: ~2.9% + 30¢ ([per same sources](https://www.fyresite.com/how-much-does-an-ecommerce-website-cost-to-develop/)). Hosting itself is cheap ($0–25/mo on Vercel/Render/Fly). **Verdict: massive overkill for a small school store; key-person risk when the volunteer/parent developer leaves.**
- **WordPress + WooCommerce:** software free; realistic DIY total **$500–1,500/yr** (hosting ~$250/yr, domain, premium plugins); professionally maintained: retainers **$500–3,000/mo** ([elementor cost guide](https://elementor.com/blog/how-much-does-a-woocommerce-store-cost/), [rovela maintenance numbers](https://rovela.ai/blog/woocommerce-maintenance-cost), [codeable](https://www.codeable.io/blog/woocommerce-development-cost/)). Membership/password plugins give gating; infinitely extensible (forms, events, donations) — but the school owns updates, security patches, and breakage.
- **Airtable + Softr:** Softr Basic **$49/mo**, Pro $139/mo; free tier = 10 app users ([Softr pricing via capterra/softr blog](https://www.softr.io/blog/softr-vs-airtable-pricing), [hackceleration](https://hackceleration.com/labs/softr-pricing)); Airtable's own Portals add-on runs $120+/mo ([noloco Airtable pricing guide](https://noloco.io/blog/airtable-pricing)). Fine for gated order forms + Stripe checkout links; weak as a real store (no true cart/inventory sync at low tiers). Costs more than Shopify Basic for less commerce.

## Top 5 takeaways

1. **Shopify Basic (~$29–39/mo) is the strongest pure-store option** for school-managed inventory: native storewide password, local pickup, real inventory, cheap gating apps — but it will never do tuition/forms, so it stays a parallel system.
2. **Square Online's free tier is the cheapest credible start** and Square uniquely expands into front-office payments (invoices, 1% ACH, POS) — but its inability to password-protect the main shop pages is a real gap for a parents-only store.
3. **If the school already runs FACTS or TADS, incidental billing may make a separate store unnecessary for required items** (post charges to family accounts, ACH free) — the gap is shopping UX (browsing, sizes, variants), which is exactly what a light storefront would add.
4. **Vendor-fulfilled stores (SquadLocker, 1st Place, BSN) cost nothing and remove all inventory labor** for spirit wear/athletic apparel, returning ~5–15% — pair one with a small school-run store for school-provided/required items rather than choosing between them.
5. **Avoid Weebly (sunsetting), custom builds ($8K–20K + maintenance for a store measured in dozens of orders), and heavy low-code stacks** — and note two pricing uncertainties to confirm on official pages: Ecwid's March 2026 repricing and conflicting reports of new Wix transaction fees.
