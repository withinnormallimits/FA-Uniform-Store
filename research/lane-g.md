# Lane G — Inventory Reality

Research date: 2026-07-23. Sources labeled: [OFFICIAL] platform docs, [MERCHANT] firsthand/community, [VENDOR] vendor marketing/blog. Staleness (>18 mo) and non-US flagged.

## Stock handling (hide vs show, notifications, oversell risk)

- Consensus: don't delete/hide sold-out product pages entirely — keep page live, add "Notify me," demote in collections, suggest alternatives; hiding hurts SEO and repeat traffic. [VENDOR — Shopify-app blogs] https://egnition.io/resources/inventory-mgmt/should-you-hide-your-out-of-stock-products ; https://www.digismoothie.com/blog/managing-sold-out-inventory ; https://www.stoqapp.com/blog/how-to-hide-out-of-stock-products-and-variants-on-shopify
- Cited stat (vendor-repeated): stockouts push ~69% of shoppers to a competitor — treat as directional, not audited. [VENDOR] https://www.digismoothie.com/blog/managing-sold-out-inventory
- Rule of thumb from these guides: use back-in-stock alerts when restock timing is uncertain; use preorder when restock date is known. [VENDOR] https://egnition.io/resources/inventory-mgmt/top-tips-for-managing-out-of-stock-items-on-shopify
- Shopify: per-variant settings "Track quantity" + "Continue selling when out of stock"; enabling the latter deliberately allows negative inventory (backorder mode). Does NOT apply to POS orders. [OFFICIAL] https://help.shopify.com/en/manual/products/inventory/setup/selling-when-out-of-stock ; explainer [VENDOR] https://www.webgility.com/blog/shopify-inventory-policy
- Wix: back-in-stock notifications are built in — "Add to Cart" becomes "Notify When Available"; requests logged in dashboard; setup is desktop-only. [OFFICIAL] https://support.wix.com/en/article/wix-stores-notifying-customers-when-products-are-back-in-stock ; https://www.wix.com/blog/back-in-stock-pre-alerts-with-wix-ecommerce
- Square Online: NO native back-in-stock customer notification — long-standing feature request; workaround is preorder mode or "PRE-ORDER" in item name. [MERCHANT — Square community; thread may be >18 mo, STALE risk] https://community.squareup.com/t5/Closed-Requests-read-only/Feature-Request-Backorder-of-specific-items/idi-p/356748
- Oversell risk is real when counts drift: POS/spreadsheet/online counts diverging, plus race conditions at checkout (see next section). General prevention: single source of truth, sync frequency, buffer stock. [VENDOR] https://www.zenventory.com/blog/how-to-prevent-overselling ; https://www.shopify.com/blog/overselling

## Preorders / backorders

- Order-then-bulk-buy ("preorder window") is now the dominant model for school spirit wear: store opens ~10–14 days (2–3 weeks max), firm close date, items produced/ordered after close, delivered in one sorted batch — zero inventory risk, no leftovers. [VENDOR but reflects common practice] https://www.mychesco.com/a/news/business/why-preorder-spirit-wear-stores-are-replacing-traditional-school-fundraisers-in-chester-county/ ; https://www.frenchpresscustom.com/blog/how-to-set-up-a-school-spirit-wear-store-step-by-step ; https://selectspiritwear.com/pages/create-school-spiritwear-store
- Shopify: native pre-orders exist via "purchase options" (deferred purchase). Charge-upfront works simply; deferred-charge vaults the card and requires Shopify Payments or PayPal Express; one due date per order; some discounts unsupported. Apps (PreProduct, STOQ, etc.) add comms/partial payment. [OFFICIAL] https://help.shopify.com/en/manual/products/purchase-options/pre-orders/setup ; https://shopify.dev/docs/apps/build/purchase-options/deferred ; overview [VENDOR] https://preproduct.io/how-to-do-pre-orders-on-shopify/
- Square Online: preorders with availability dates supported, but only on Plus/Premium plans and only for Pickup and Local Delivery — NOT shipping. [OFFICIAL] https://squareup.com/help/us/en/article/8285-sell-items-as-preorders-with-square-online ; [MERCHANT] https://community.squareup.com/t5/Online-Store/Pre-Order-Out-of-stock-items/m-p/791502
- Wix: preorder for out-of-stock products supported natively (not available in Spaces-by-Wix member app). [OFFICIAL] https://support.wix.com/en/article/wix-stores-request-pre-ordering-products-in-spaces-by-wix
- ETA communication: guides stress publishing expected-delivery date on the product page and in confirmation email; deferred-payment UX guidelines require clear "charged later on X / ships by Y" language at checkout. [OFFICIAL] https://shopify.dev/docs/themes/pricing-payments/purchase-options/deferred-ux-guidelines
- Backorder mode (sell into negative stock) is the low-tech alternative on Shopify via "Continue selling when out of stock" — but nothing tells the buyer it's backordered unless you edit the page. [OFFICIAL] https://help.shopify.com/en/manual/products/inventory/setup/selling-when-out-of-stock

## Checkout reservation (who gets the last item)

- Shopify does NOT reserve items in the cart; adding to cart holds nothing. Inventory is checked/decremented around payment completion. [MERCHANT explainer] https://www.krakengolf.com/blogs/the-deep-cut-where-golf-metal-and-story-run-deeper/why-your-cart-doesnt-reserve-a-drop-the-truth-about-shopify-and-limited-releases ; [MERCHANT, STALE — old community threads] https://community.shopify.com/t/cart-hold-hold-products-in-inventory-guaranteed-for-x-minutes/161970
- Race condition: two buyers can both pass the stock check on the last unit — especially with external/offsite payment gateways, where Shopify can't decrement until the buyer returns from the gateway; both transactions succeed and inventory goes negative. Known, documented-in-community limitation. [MERCHANT, likely STALE thread but behavior repeatedly reconfirmed] https://community.shopify.com/t/oversold-inventory-2-customers-buy-at-the-same-time/128392
- Manual reservation on Shopify = Draft Orders: reserving flips inventory to "unavailable" for a merchant-set time, then releases. Good for "hold this for a parent" cases. [VENDOR how-to] https://www.hulkapps.com/blogs/shopify-hub/maximize-your-shopify-store-efficiency-with-draft-order-to-reserve-items ; https://egnition.io/resources/customer-xp/how-to-reserve-items-for-customers-in-your-shopify-store
- Contrast: Stripe Checkout documents an explicit merchant-managed pattern (decrement on session create, 30-min expiry, restock on expire) — shows reservation is a design choice platforms make differently. [OFFICIAL] https://stripe.com/docs/payments/checkout/managing-limited-inventory
- Practical implication for a rush: expect occasional oversells on the last unit of a size; plan a refund-and-apologize or backorder-and-fulfill-late script rather than assuming the platform prevents it.

## Pickup vs shipping

- Shopify local pickup: native "pickup in store" delivery method; marking order "ready" auto-sends a Ready-for-pickup email; pickup instructions field customizable (Settings > Notifications). [OFFICIAL] https://help.shopify.com/en/manual/fulfillment/setup/delivery-methods/pickup-in-store ; https://help.shopify.com/en/manual/taxes/shopify-tax/notifications/ready-for-pickup ; POS-side flow https://help.shopify.com/en/manual/sell-in-person/shopify-pos/order-management/pickup-in-store-for-online-orders
- Square Online: pickup + local delivery are the fulfillment types preorders support (fits a front-office pickup model without shipping at all). [OFFICIAL] https://squareup.com/help/us/en/article/8285-sell-items-as-preorders-with-square-online
- School distribution patterns observed in the wild: (a) sent home with student / backpack delivery for lower grades; (b) student pickup at a campus location for upper grades; (c) office pickup; (d) optional home shipping at parent cost; batch delivery to school ~monthly, sorted by grade/teacher or packed per student. [MERCHANT/school pages] https://www.ashleyhall.org/spiritstore/ ; https://saintjamesschool.net/sjs-spiritwear-orders/ ; https://www.ckschool.net/parents/order-school-uniforms-merchandise.cfm ; https://www.mcchristianschool.com/student-life/store/ ; vendor pack-per-student service [VENDOR] https://varsityvault.io/10-best-print-on-demand-spirit-shops-for-teams-and-boosters/
- Shipping cost reality (US, current): USPS Ground Advantage commercial for a shirt-weight package roughly $4–8.50 depending on zone; July 12, 2026 change eliminates sub-1-lb ounce tiers (all 1–15.999 oz priced at the old 12–15.999 oz rate) and raises commercial rates ~11.8% — lightweight apparel shipping just got notably more expensive, strengthening the case for free school pickup. [NEWS/analysis] https://www.shipsaving.com/blog/usps-july-2026-update-what-shippers-need-to-know ; https://transimpact.com/blog/usps-rate-to-increase-ground-advantage-commercial-rates-by-11.8 ; https://atoship.com/blog/usps-shipping-rates-2026

## Physical ops (counts, shrinkage, receiving, storage)

- Cycle counts beat annual full counts for small stores: count a small slice on a schedule (weekly/monthly), high-value/fast movers most often (ABC method); barcode scanners + standardized count sheets cut errors. [VENDOR guides] https://www.shopify.com/blog/cycle-count ; https://fitsmallbusiness.com/cycle-count/ ; https://www.posnation.com/blog/cycle-counts-best-practices
- Shrinkage sources: theft, damage, admin/count error; regular counting is the primary detection tool; one study cited shows tech-supported cycle counting raising accuracy 72%→99.4%. [VENDOR] https://www.lightspeedhq.com/blog/inventory-shrinkage-what-it-is-and-how-to-prevent-it/ ; https://www.stockount.com/articles/inventory-shrinkage-retail-loss-prevention-audit-system
- Volunteer-run reality: PTA uniform shops run on donations + parent volunteers, often no backstock discipline, and volunteers report being buried in unsold stock — expect informal processes and drift. [MERCHANT accounts, NON-US (UK)] https://www.pta.co.uk/fundraising/ideas/second-hand-uniform/ ; https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/_chat/3715830-school-second-hand-uniform-sales-pta ; US example (donation-only, everything out for sale, no backstock) https://allsaintsvaschool.org/parent-resources/all-saints-pto/uniform-closet/
- Receiving bulk vendor orders: check packing slip against PO line by line before signing; document shortages/overages (photos); in POS systems (e.g., Lightspeed) stock isn't added until items are checked in — partial receiving supported. [VENDOR/OFFICIAL] https://www.shopify.com/blog/receiving-inventory ; https://retail-support.lightspeedhq.com/hc/en-us/articles/8153229791131-Receiving-items-in-purchase-orders ; https://www.finaleinventory.com/guides/goods-receipt/
- Storage constraint mitigation: the preorder model exists precisely because schools lack storage — goods arrive pre-sorted in one batch and go straight to distribution. [VENDOR] https://www.mychesco.com/a/news/business/why-preorder-spirit-wear-stores-are-replacing-traditional-school-fundraisers-in-chester-county/

## Seasonal spikes (back-to-school)

- The spike is narrow and predictable: heavy family buying roughly Aug 5–20; winners have catalog, stock rules, and vendor pipelines configured by ~July 1; planning starts in May. [VENDOR] https://flxpoint.com/blog/back-to-school-ecommerce-checklist ; https://flxpoint.com/blog/survive-the-back-to-school-rush
- Lead-time math: a "3-week production" custom SKU is really 60–90 days PO-to-sellable; for July 1 stock the PO must go out by late April. [VENDOR] https://flxpoint.com/blog/back-to-school-ecommerce-checklist
- Publish cutoff dates early and clearly — "order by X to receive by first day" is the single most practical spike-management tool. [VENDOR] https://www.flatoutfulfillment.com/post/essential-steps-to-prepare-your-business-for-peak-shipping-seasons
- Staged/windowed releases: seasonal store windows (2–3 weeks pre-school-year, plus homecoming/sports-season reopenings) vs always-on core collection; company-store ops guides frame the core decision as preorder-window vs stock-on-hand, with deadlines and shipping mode chosen per launch. [VENDOR] https://selectspiritwear.com/pages/create-school-spiritwear-store ; https://bridgeprinting.com/promotional-products/seasonal-company-store-launch-ops/
- General seasonal prep for small shops: forecast from last season's sell-through per size/SKU, avoid over-buying (post-season leftovers are the main downside of stock-on-hand), and per-location ordering plans. [VENDOR] https://www.ideafinancial.com/blog/strategic-seasonal-inventory-planning-how-small-businesses-can-prepare-for-peak-sales-periods ; https://www.lightspeedhq.com/blog/back-to-school-promotions/

## Top 5 takeaways

1. No mainstream platform reserves cart inventory — two parents CAN both buy the last youth-M polo during a rush (worst with external payment gateways). Plan a graceful oversell script (refund or backorder) instead of assuming prevention.
2. The preorder window (open 2–3 weeks, hard close, bulk-order after, batch-distribute) is the proven model for school stores: it eliminates stock risk, storage problems, and size-guessing — at the cost of wait time, so publish ETAs and cutoffs prominently.
3. Platform fit matters: Shopify has native preorders + back-in-stock via cheap apps + polished local-pickup/ready-email flow; Square preorders need Plus/Premium and only work for pickup/local delivery (no shipping) and lack back-in-stock alerts; Wix has back-in-stock built in.
4. Local pickup (front office / backpack delivery sorted by teacher) should be the default fulfillment — USPS's July 2026 repricing makes shipping a single shirt ~$5–8+, a big share of item price.
5. For whatever is stocked on hand: keep sold-out pages visible with "notify me," run small monthly cycle counts on fast movers (volunteer-run stores drift badly without them), and check every vendor delivery against the PO before it hits the shelf.
