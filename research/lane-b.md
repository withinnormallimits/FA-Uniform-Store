# Lane B — School Operations

How small US private and parochial schools actually run uniform/supply stores day to day. Researched July 2026. Vendor marketing is labeled; non-US sources are flagged.

## Staffing: who actually runs it

- The dominant model at parochial schools is a PTO/parents-club used-uniform program run entirely by parent volunteers, who sort, fold, and size donated items and set up sale tables at school events; frequency is explicitly capped by "as often as the PTO can source volunteers." (All Saints Catholic School PTO, VA) https://allsaintsvaschool.org/parent-resources/all-saints-pto/uniform-closet/
- Some schools designate a single "Store Coordinator" (parent volunteer) with a dedicated store email (e.g., studentstore@serraschool.org), who recruits other parents for shifts and "online fulfillment teams." (St. Junipero Serra Catholic, CA) https://www.serraschool.org/parents/serraspiritstore.cfm
- Front office staff are routinely the intake point: donations "accepted at the front office throughout the school year and during summer hours," and money from rack sales is handed to the school secretary. https://allsaintsvaschool.org/parent-resources/all-saints-pto/uniform-closet/ ; https://classic.ptotoday.com/boards/25-involvement-ideas-and-challenges/111680-uniform-closet (PTO Today forum, firsthand accounts; thread is old — possibly stale)
- Volunteer-run resale shops also exist at public/charter schools (e.g., South Loop Elementary's volunteer-run resale shop, all proceeds to the school), so the pattern is not exclusive to private schools. https://southloopschool.org/shop/
- Non-US comparison (flag: Australia): P&C uniform-shop committee role descriptions list stock ordering, checking incoming stock for faults, supplier liaison, online store updates, twice-yearly stocktakes, and fitting-appointment rosters at ~1–2 hrs/week — a rare written job description of the coordinator role, largely transferable. https://sites.google.com/site/enpsuniformshop/uniform-shop-committee
- High schools sometimes run a staff/campus-ministry "clothing exchange closet" for students out of uniform, a distinct staffing model from PTO resale (Jesuit High School, Sacramento). https://www.jesuithighschool.org/plank-article/clothing-exchange-closet-helps-students-stay-uniform

## Fulfillment: how orders reach families

- On-campus store windows are short and fixed: Serra's store opens Tue 7:30–8:30am and Thu 1:00–2:15pm plus appointments — i.e., a couple hours a week, appointment-driven for fittings and used-uniform shopping. https://www.serraschool.org/student-life/studentstore.cfm
- Online spirit-wear/uniform orders are commonly distributed to the student at school: DeBakey HS PTO has students pick up orders at lunch weekly and sign for receipt; Ben Franklin Academy delivers to the student by default with a "mark as gift" option to reroute to front-office pickup. https://www.debakeypto.org/uniformstore ; https://www.bfacademy.org/the-ptos-spirit-wear-store-is-open/
- Classroom/"backpack" delivery is a standard option in platform-run stores; some schools deliver online uniform orders to the child's classroom within a week (flag: Picton example is Australian, but US platforms offer the same school-delivery batching). https://picton-p.schools.nsw.gov.au/news/2018/11/welcoming--the-school-locker-.html ; https://selectspiritwear.com/pages/create-school-spiritwear-store (vendor marketing)
- Spirit-wear platforms (vendor marketing) pitch batch fulfillment "packaged individually for easy distribution" at school vs direct-to-home shipping, run as periodic store "open/close" windows rather than always-on inventory. https://www.vancopayments.com/education/school-spirit-wear-online-store ; https://www.bfacademy.org/events/pto-spirit-wear-store-is-open-718-176-130-193/
- Outsourced vendors fulfill via retail-store fittings by appointment plus home shipping (Allen's School Uniforms, MA: fittings by appointment only; ~2 weeks for online delivery). https://www.allensuniforms.com/schools/

## Inventory: storage and counting

- Used-uniform inventory typically lives in marginal school space: a hanging rack in the "sick room" with folded shorts on top, a closet, or gym storage, opened during school hours or on sale days. https://classic.ptotoday.com/boards/25-involvement-ideas-and-challenges/111680-uniform-closet (old thread — possibly stale) ; https://www.smsva.org/parents/on-campus/uniforms/uniform-exchangelost-and-found
- A common anti-inventory policy: put every donated item out at every sale and keep no backstock, which eliminates counting entirely. https://allsaintsvaschool.org/parent-resources/all-saints-pto/uniform-closet/
- Intake quality control is the main inventory gate: only laundered items free of stains, rips, or excessive fading are accepted; damaged donations are discarded or recycled. https://www.smsva.org/parents/on-campus/uniforms/uniform-exchangelost-and-found ; https://handmeupshop.com/blogs/news/ideas-for-how-to-set-up-a-uniform-closet-at-your-school (vendor blog)
- Sorting/prep is done by volunteers, often at home before sale day; best practice is racks/hangers by type and size with laminated price lists, not piles (flag: Milton Keynes guide is UK, but practices transferable). https://www.pinelakeprep.org/pto/events-and-fundraisers/used-uniform-sales ; https://www.milton-keynes.gov.uk/sites/default/files/2022-02/Advice%20on%20preloved%20school%20uniform.pdf
- Where real stock is held (new goods), formal counts are rare and periodic — the written standard found was twice-yearly stocktakes by the volunteer coordinator (flag: Australian P&C source). https://sites.google.com/site/enpsuniformshop/uniform-shop-committee
- Credit-based exchanges reduce cash and inventory pressure: 1 credit per donated item redeemable for used clothing (Serra), or straight one-for-one swaps (donate two polos, take two polos). https://www.serraschool.org/student-life/studentstore.cfm ; https://archangelgabrielpgh.org/uniform-exchange

## Payments and money handling

- Small sales are still heavily cash/check with informal handling (money left with the school secretary); modern sales add Square/Venmo/PayPal — Great Hearts Irving's used-uniform sale accepts debit, credit, Venmo, PayPal, check, and cash and asks for exact change. https://classic.ptotoday.com/boards/25-involvement-ideas-and-challenges/111680-uniform-closet (old — possibly stale) ; https://irving.greatheartsamerica.org/useduniformsale/
- PTO money-handling best practice: two people count and sign off before deposit, deposit within 24 hours, lock cash in the school safe if deposit is delayed, never let one person receive/record/deposit. https://www.ptotoday.com/pto-today-articles/financial-best-practices-school-pto-pta-groups-pto-treasurer-tips ; https://www.ptotoday.com/pto-today-articles/article/391-handling-cash-6-simple-steps
- Diocesan finance manuals impose parish-level internal controls on school stores: pre-numbered duplicate receipts, no single employee receiving+recording+depositing, locked safe storage, two-person counts (Archdiocese of Chicago; USCCB framework; Diocese of Grand Rapids policy manual covers "monies in school buildings" and petty cash). https://www.archchicago.org/documents/70111/1068401/Parish+Best+Practices+for+Internal+Controls.pdf ; https://www.usccb.org/about/financial-reporting/diocesan-internal-controls-framework.cfm ; https://grdiocese.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/10/report-office-of-catholic-schools-policy-manual-with-table-of-contents.pdf
- Consignment splits are a standard pattern for used-uniform sales: 50/50 family/HSA (Mary of Nazareth), 80/20 consignor/PTF (Prince Avenue Christian), or donation-for-credit instead of cash. https://www.maryofnazareth.org/used-uniform-sale ; https://princeave.org/current-families/gently-used-uniform-sale/ ; https://uniformee.com/LearnMore/ (vendor)
- PTO embezzlement-prevention guidance exists precisely because these all-volunteer cash operations are a known theft risk. https://ptoanswers.com/prevent-pto-theft/

## Seasonal spikes: back-to-school

- The workload peak is a compressed July window: multi-day drop-off then sale events (Prince Avenue: drop-off Mon July 20, sale Tue July 22 with a new-student-exclusive hour), Hockaday resale boutique Sat July 11, Silverdale Baptist public sale July 17, New Covenant July 28 — all staffed by parent (and student) volunteers recruited via sign-ups. https://princeave.org/current-families/gently-used-uniform-sale/ ; https://www.hockaday.org/news-detail?pk=1362499 ; https://parents.silverdaleba.org/parent-dashboard/school-uniforms/ptf-uniform-resale ; https://www.newcovenantschools.org/2026/05/29/summer-uniform-sales/
- Vendors mirror the spike with hard deadlines: orders after ~July 31 "cannot be guaranteed for the start of school," and summer fittings are appointment-only (Allen's, MA; flag: Peter Spivey is UK with same July 31 cutoff). https://www.allensuniforms.com/schools/ ; https://www.peterspivey.com/
- Sales are timed to piggyback on existing traffic: registration day, back-to-school night, curriculum night, and fall/spring conferences, with a fall+spring minimum cadence. https://www.svscatholic.org/campus-life/uniform-exchange/ ; https://allsaintsvaschool.org/parent-resources/all-saints-pto/uniform-closet/
- Off-peak, some schools run a low-effort steady state instead: monthly sales on the gym stage (Saint Andrew Catholic) or a year-round closet open first Monday of the month. https://www.standrewsch.org/inside-sas/uniformresale.cfm ; https://classic.ptotoday.com/boards/25-involvement-ideas-and-challenges/111680-uniform-closet (old — possibly stale)

## Failure modes and transitions

- Vendor concentration risk is real: Dennis Uniform, the largest US school-uniform maker (~500,000 students, 104 years old), filed bankruptcy and shut all 22 stores overnight in Oct 2024; schools were never notified and learned from parents, then scrambled for new suppliers (event >18 months old but a durable lesson). https://roughdraftatlanta.com/2024/10/26/school-uniform-store-closes-unexpectedly-in-sandy-springs/ ; https://www.kens5.com/article/money/business/school-uniform-store-abruptly-shuts-doors/273-7ae45ff1-5440-4c8c-a175-a096f041dda7 ; https://thebuzzmagazines.com/articles/2024/12/awtys-transition-bankrupt-uniform-provider
- Post-Dennis, competitors published vendor-selection guides and takeover offers — evidence schools had no documented fallback plan (vendor marketing). https://skobels.com/blogs/news/the-ultimate-guide-to-selecting-a-new-school-uniform-vendor-navigating-changes-after-dennis-uniforms-closure ; https://flynnohara.com/dennis-closure-response/
- Volunteer-run programs degrade to their volunteer supply: exchanges run "as often as the PTO can source volunteers," with twice a year as the floor — no volunteers, no store. https://allsaintsvaschool.org/parent-resources/all-saints-pto/uniform-closet/
- When the responsible person leaves, knowledge loss is the known failure: PTO guidance prescribes a transition binder, early handoff before year-end (outgoing treasurers become unreachable), and shared Google Drive access for the incoming leader. https://ptoanswers.com/pto-leader-transition-best-practices/ ; https://moneyminder.com/blog/pto-pta-booster-club-treasurer-transitions-free-guide-year-end-checklist/ ; https://parentswhovolunteer.com/pta-binder-what-to-include-in-each-section/
- Cash-control failure (theft/embezzlement) is common enough that "keep your PTO out of the news" guidance targets principals specifically. https://ptoanswers.com/secure-pto-money-tips/

## Outsourcing vs in-house

- Outsourced model (vendor marketing): FlynnO'Hara partners with 1,300+ private schools, offers up to 10% rebate on uniform sales back to the school, on-campus pop-up measuring/order events, faculty discounts, and hardship assistance — the school holds no inventory and handles no payments. https://flynnohara.com/become-a-partner/ ; https://flynnohara.com/why-us/ ; https://ourcompany.flynnohara.com/school-fundraising/
- Lands' End School runs school-specific online storefronts of approved styles/colors shipped direct to home — zero school-side fulfillment. https://www.landsend.com/shop/school/S-ytp-xe8 (vendor marketing)
- The common hybrid: new uniforms outsourced to a vendor (Serra restricts donations to "Uniforms4U or Lands' End only" items), while the school/PTO keeps only used-uniform resale and spirit wear in-house. https://www.serraschool.org/student-life/studentstore.cfm
- NAIS/NBOA framing: campus stores sit inside "auxiliary programs," a small slice of budget (~4.4% of revenue) but with revenue per student growing faster than cost (12.8% vs 5.4%) — schools are told to treat auxiliaries as managed businesses, not afterthoughts. https://www.nboa.org/net-assets/article/nothing-but-net ; https://www.sparcnational.com/news/how-auxiliary-bucks-the-trend-in-independent-school-revenue
- Non-US comparison (flag: Australia): a formal P&C uniform-shop review weighing volunteer-run in-house vs outsourcing exists but was not retrievable (403); the pattern of periodic in-house-vs-outsource reviews is transferable. https://concord-h.schools.nsw.gov.au/content/dam/doe/sws/schools/c/concord-h/p-and-c/Report_Uniform_Shop_Review_May_2019.pdf

## Top 5 takeaways

1. The de facto operating model is a two-track system: new uniforms outsourced to a vendor (rebate-funded, appointment fittings, home shipping), with an in-house volunteer-run used-uniform closet/resale handling everything else — few small schools stock new goods themselves.
2. The store is only as reliable as its volunteers and its binder: sale frequency is explicitly rationed by volunteer supply, and PTO guidance treats transition binders/early handoffs as the only defense against knowledge loss when a coordinator leaves.
3. Money handling is the highest-risk process: cash/check still dominates small sales, and both PTO best practice and diocesan finance manuals converge on two-person counts, pre-numbered receipts, 24-hour deposits, and never letting one person receive+record+deposit.
4. Fulfillment defaults to the school as the last mile: lunch-time/front-office pickup, classroom or backpack delivery of batched online orders, and short fixed store hours (often ~2 hrs/week) with appointments for fittings.
5. Everything concentrates in July: drop-off/sale events, vendor order-by-July-31 deadlines, and registration-day/back-to-school-night sales create a hard seasonal spike — and the Dennis Uniform overnight collapse (Oct 2024) shows schools need a documented vendor fallback plan.
