# Lane F — Uniform Domain

What makes selling school uniforms different from generic apparel e-commerce. Researched July 2026. Sources labeled: (vendor) = vendor policy/marketing page, (school) = school handbook/site as pattern example, (secondary) = blog/aggregator. Stale (>18 mo) and non-US findings flagged.

## Sizing

- Uniform vendors carry an unusually wide size matrix in one program: K-12 regular plus extended fits — tall, slim, husky/plus, junior — so one polo style can span 15+ sizes. (vendor) https://www.landsend.com/schooladmin/faq/ ; Lands' End publishes a dedicated School "Fit Guide" PDF with separate charts for kids regular, kids slim, and kids husky/plus. (vendor) https://www.landsend.com/schooladmin/pdf/LESU_Fit_Guide.pdf
- Lands' End tells parents a child "may need a different size in slim or plus than their regular size" — fit variant is a separate axis, not just a bigger number. Kohl's hosts the same brand chart with regular/plus-husky/slim tables side by side. (vendor) https://www.kohls.com/media/digital/ecom/size-charts/childrens/html/KohlsLandsEndNeutralKidsSizeChart.html
- Online fit uncertainty is mitigated with recommendation tools: Lands' End pushes True Fit (answer questions about the child, get a size recommendation). (vendor) https://www.landsend.com/schooladmin/faq/
- Youth/adult overlap is a real trap: Youth XL and Adult Small share chest width (~34-36 in) but Adult S is ~3 in longer in the body; youth cuts are shorter/narrower in shoulders. Kids typically cross over between ages 14-16. (secondary) https://theloyalbrand.com/blogs/blog/is-youth-xl-the-same-as-adult-small ; https://theloyalbrand.com/blogs/blog/mastering-the-size-shift-from-youth-xl-to-adult-small-shirts
- Fit/durability guarantees are a selling point: DENNIS guarantees garments against one calendar year of normal wear and lifetime against manufacturer defects. (vendor) https://help.dennisuniform.com/hc/en-us/articles/1500010627801-What-is-DENNIS-Uniform-s-return-policy — note: DENNIS entered liquidation (its storefront pages are branded "Liquidation Sale"), so treat as a pattern example, possibly stale. https://www.dennisuniform.com/pages/return-policy
- Lands' End markets that returns are accepted "even embroidered with a logo" and regardless of wash/wear in its school sales material — but see Returns below for the actual current window. (vendor, marketing PDF, likely stale >18 mo) https://bradfordchristianacademy.org/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/us_deluxe_packet.pdf

## Returns / exchanges

- Flynn O'Hara: exchanges/refunds up to 180 days, but only unworn, unwashed, unaltered, original tags attached — the long window exists because parents buy in July for a September start. (vendor) https://flynnohara.com/shipping-returns/ ; https://stratfordschools.flynnohara.com/policies/refund-policy
- DENNIS: 90-day return with receipt for refund/store credit; item must still be "current on your school's uniform program" (a uniform-specific wrinkle — returns tied to whether the school still allows the item). Non-returnable: "All Sales Final/While Supplies Last" items, hair accessories, custom-made garments, hem extensions, bulk orders. Original condition (unwashed, unworn, tags on) required. (vendor) https://help.dennisuniform.com/hc/en-us/articles/1500010627801-What-is-DENNIS-Uniform-s-return-policy ; https://help.dennisuniform.com/hc/en-us/articles/23089363390871-Are-all-items-eligible-for-returns
- Lands' End: famous "Guaranteed. Period." policy has been narrowed — general returns 90 days; crucially, school-logo items are returnable within 90 days for merchandise credit only, and hemming + school uniform logos are explicitly NOT considered "personalization" (personalized monogram/embroidery items are final sale). (secondary — vendor policy page blocked WebFetch 403, verify wording on landsend.com) https://thereturnguide.com/blogs/retail-store-returns/lands-end-return-policy-guide ; https://donotpay.com/learn/lands-end-return-policy/ ; https://www.printfriendly.com/document/lands-end-school-uniform-return-policy-overview
- French Toast: clearance items marked "final sale, no returns or exchanges"; logo/heat-transfer decorated items are "custom decorated products… custom made for you" taking 10-14 business days — the framing vendors use to justify limited returns on decorated goods. (vendor pages; frenchtoast.com blocked direct fetch, wording via search snippets — verify) https://www.frenchtoast.com/customer-service/returns
- Hygiene norm across the category: no returns on worn or washed garments (Flynn O'Hara, DENNIS both require unworn/unwashed); accessories like hair items excluded entirely (DENNIS). (vendor) links above.
- Back-to-school timing: one uniform retailer recommends ordering embroidered items at least 6 weeks before term start specifically "to allow time for exchanges"; peak-season deadlines in July guarantee first-day delivery. (mixed US/UK — Peter Spivey and YourSchoolUniform are UK; flag non-US) https://yourschooluniform.com/p/customer-services/delivery/ ; https://www.peterspivey.com/ ; US example: https://www.myschoolsuniform.com/service/shipping-returns/

## Embroidery / decoration

- Decorated-to-order lead times: French Toast custom logo embroidery/heat transfer takes 10-14 business days per order (vendor) https://www.frenchtoast.com/customer-service/returns ; bulk program: 300-unit minimum for decorated items, 200 undecorated, 400 mixed, up to 6 weeks lead time. (vendor) https://www.frenchtoast.com/bulk-uniforms
- Small-shop counterpoint: Oak & Twine advertises 8-piece embroidery minimums with mid-season reorders (vendor) https://www.oakandtwine.com/page/blog/why-use-oak-and-twine-for-your-school-uniforms ; Corporate Casuals ships embroidered school polos in 3-4 business days, no order minimum positioning. (vendor) https://www.corporatecasuals.com/embroidery/school-uniforms.htm
- In-house decoration as differentiator: Flynn O'Hara equips each retail location with embroidery and silk-screen machines to apply school logos on site; Educational Outfitters (MN) does all decoration in house. (vendor) https://flynnohara.com/faq/ ; https://minnesota.educationaloutfitters.com/
- Logos sold as SKUs: Flynn O'Hara lists the school logo emblem itself as a catalog item tied to a school code (e.g., PA021-EMBLEM). (vendor) https://flynnohara.com/school-logo-pa021-emblem-n-a/
- Screen print vs embroidery economics (2025-2026 figures): screen print ~$3-12/piece ($3-5 at 100+ units), setup $50-200 per color/screen, wins on volume; embroidery ~$5-18/piece (left-chest logo $5-8), digitization setup $30-100, wins on small batches and premium look (polos, outerwear). Embroidery is the school-uniform default for chest logos; screen print for PE/spiritwear. (secondary) https://arklavo.com/blogs/custom-apparel-guide/screen-print-vs-embroidery-guide ; https://www.gotapparel.com/blog/custom-embroidery-vs-screen-printing-which-method-is-right-for-your-apparel/ ; https://customteesnow.com/embroidery-vs-sublimation-vs-screen-printing-cost/
- Seasonality: embroidery turnaround roughly doubles in summer — ~2 weeks off-season vs 3-4 weeks in back-to-school peak; recommended ordering window 4-6 weeks out. (secondary) https://kustomimprints.com/what-is-the-best-time-to-order-custom-school-apparel/
- Final-sale implication: custom-decorated = custom-made is the industry rationale for excluding decorated items from returns (DENNIS "custom-made garments" non-returnable; general industry practice). (vendor) https://help.dennisuniform.com/hc/en-us/articles/23089363390871-Are-all-items-eligible-for-returns ; (secondary) https://www.enzuzo.com/blog/all-sales-are-final-policy — notable exception: Lands' End treats school logos as NOT personalization and takes them back for credit (see Returns).

## Catalog restrictions (grade / gender / season)

- The dress code document is the source of truth, and vendors encode it: Lands' End assigns each school a 9-digit "Preferred School Number"; entering it filters the whole catalog to only that school's approved items and auto-offers the school's logo. (vendor) https://www.landsend.com/article/understanding-lands-end-school-uniform-program/ ; example per-school PDFs generated from the code: https://www.landsend.com/pdfs/school/900126053.pdf
- French Toast does the same with "Shop By School" — parents find their school and see only its approved list. (vendor) https://www.frenchtoast.com/shop-by-school
- Grade x gender restrictions are the norm, not the exception: Saint Rose (San Jose) — girls K-5 wear tartan plaid jumper/skirt; different rules for upper grades. (school) https://saintroseonline.org/school/resources/dress-code ; St. Paul (KY) — plaid jumper grades K-4, plaid skirt grades 4-8; boys' pants specs split K-5 vs upper. (school) https://stpaulnky.org/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2011/05/STUDENT-DRESS-CODE-2016-2017.doc (stale, 2016 — pattern example only)
- Some schools make the vendor exclusive: "Only clothes purchased through Lands' End will be considered appropriate dress." (school) https://www.fcsva.com/hubfs/Dress%20Code%20updated%207.12.21.pdf (2021 — stale, pattern example)
- Seasonal rules appear in handbooks (shorts/skorts only in warm months, sweaters in winter); Mater Dei Academy publishes a dated dress-code PDF per school year — codes change annually, so an item "current on the uniform program" this year may not be next year (DENNIS's return policy explicitly conditions on this). (school) https://www.materdeiacademy.us/images/stories/documents/Dress%20Code%20-%202025-2026.pdf ; (vendor) https://help.dennisuniform.com/hc/en-us/articles/1500010627801-What-is-DENNIS-Uniform-s-return-policy
- PE/gym uniforms are typically a separate program per grade band and sold alongside dress uniforms (school resale programs distinguish "school or gym uniforms"). (school) https://stchrisschool.org/uniformresale

## Variants / SKUs

- SKU explosion math: every size x color combo is a SKU; 5 sizes x 4 colors = 20 SKUs for one style; uniforms add a third axis (fit: regular/slim/husky) so a single boys' pant can exceed 100 combos. (secondary) https://www.shipbob.com/blog/sku-variations/ ; https://www.efulfillmentservice.com/2026/06/apparel-sku-complexity-why-size-runs-break-self-fulfillment/
- Platform limits shape store design: Shopify's variant cap was 100 per product for years; raised to 2,048 for all merchants (announced Oct 2025), but still max 3 option axes, and Liquid storefront code only sees the first 250 variants — so legacy uniform stores split colors into separate products and link them. (vendor/platform) https://shopify.dev/changelog/the-product-variant-limit-is-now-2048-for-all-merchants ; (secondary) https://rubikify.com/shopify-apparel-variant-sku-structure/ ; https://www.ilanadavis.com/blogs/articles/not-so-fast-on-shopifys-2048-variant-limit-increase
- Recommended pattern for apparel: size as native variant, color as separate products, strict SKU naming convention, combined listings to shop as one item. (secondary) https://rubikify.com/shopify-apparel-variant-sku-structure/
- Uniform-specific multiplier: the same polo may also exist decorated vs undecorated and per-school-logo, multiplying SKUs again (Flynn O'Hara sells per-school logo emblems as separate catalog items keyed to school codes). (vendor) https://flynnohara.com/school-logo-pa021-emblem-n-a/

## Used uniforms (resale / exchange)

- Parents'-association resale is a standard fixture at private schools: Hockaday (Dallas) runs an annual Uniform Resale Boutique fed by donations of gently used uniforms. (school) https://www.hockaday.org/news-detail?pk=1362499&nc=17320&fromId=234354
- Kew-Forest (NY) PA resells clean, gently used uniforms at flat $5/piece during first week of school and at conferences. (school) https://kewforest.myschoolapp.com/page/parents-association/uniform-resale
- Free-exchange model: McDonogh (MD) keeps a "Used Uniform Room" of free gently used dress-code clothing per division. (school) https://www.mcdonogh.org/community/parents/uniform-sales ; Archangel Gabriel (PA) inspects, inventories, and catalogs donated pieces; parents request from the catalog and items go home with the child. (school) https://archangelgabrielpgh.org/uniform-exchange ; ISLA PTO runs a no-cost exchange, donation not required. (school) https://pto.isla.school/what-we-do/uniform-exchange
- Buy/sell model: St. Christopher School FSA annual resale lets families buy AND sell used school or gym uniforms to each other. (school) https://stchrisschool.org/uniformresale
- Dedicated software exists for this niche: Uniformee (school-community uniform swap platform) and Schooly (mobile marketplace app for buying/selling/freecycling used uniforms, equipment, books within a school). (vendor) https://uniformee.com/LearnMore/ ; https://www.schoolyapps.com/
- Condition rules mirror retail hygiene norms: "clean, gently used," meets current dress code, inspected before listing. (school) https://pto.isla.school/what-we-do/uniform-exchange
- Secondary-market liquidity is visible on eBay (Flynn O'Hara, Lands' End husky-size uniform pieces resold individually) — but listings surfaced were on ebay.de (non-US, flag). https://www.ebay.de/itm/264570084848

## Top 5 takeaways

1. The school's dress code document — not the merchandiser — is the source of truth. Leading vendors (Lands' End Preferred School Number, French Toast Shop By School) filter the entire catalog per school, per gender, per grade band, and codes change every school year, which even affects returnability (DENNIS only takes back items still "current on your school's uniform program").
2. Decoration splits the catalog into two return regimes: blanks follow normal apparel rules (90-180 days, unworn/unwashed/tags on), while decorated-to-order items lean final-sale — with Lands' End as the notable exception that treats school logos as non-personalization (90-day merchandise credit). Decorated items also carry 10-14 business day (up to 6 week) lead times that collide with back-to-school.
3. Sizing is a three-axis problem (size x fit variant slim/husky/plus x youth-vs-adult crossover), and because parents buy weeks early for kids who are still growing, generous exchange windows (Flynn O'Hara 180 days) and fit tools (True Fit, published fit-guide PDFs) are competitive necessities, not nice-to-haves.
4. SKU explosion is worse than generic apparel: size x color x fit x decorated/undecorated x per-school logo. Model fit variants and decoration state deliberately (e.g., size as native variant, color/fit as linked products, logo as an add-on SKU) rather than one giant variant grid — even post-2025 Shopify still caps at 3 option axes and 250 Liquid-visible variants.
5. Used-uniform resale/exchange is an expected part of the private-school ecosystem (PA-run boutiques, free uniform rooms, $5 flat-price sales, swap apps like Uniformee/Schooly); a school store that ignores it misses how families actually acquire half their uniform pieces — and it implies demand for condition grading, per-school cataloging, and dress-code-currency checks on used items.
